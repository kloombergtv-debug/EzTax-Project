TAX DEDUCTIONS AND CREDITS GUIDE

DEDUCTIONS vs CREDITS
- Deductions reduce your taxable income
- Credits reduce your tax liability dollar-for-dollar

STANDARD DEDUCTION 2024
- Single: $14,600
- Married Filing Jointly: $29,200
- Married Filing Separately: $14,600
- Head of Household: $21,900

COMMON ITEMIZED DEDUCTIONS

1. Medical and Dental Expenses
- Deductible if they exceed 7.5% of AGI
- Includes medical insurance premiums, prescription drugs, medical equipment
- Transportation costs for medical care

2. State and Local Taxes (SALT)
- Limited to $10,000 ($5,000 if married filing separately)
- Includes state income tax, local income tax, property tax
- Can elect to deduct sales tax instead of income tax

3. Home Mortgage Interest
- Interest on acquisition debt up to $750,000
- Interest on home equity debt if used to improve the home
- Points paid on mortgage origination

4. Charitable Contributions
- Cash contributions: up to 60% of AGI
- Appreciated property: up to 30% of AGI
- Must be to qualified organizations
- Require proper documentation

MAJOR TAX CREDITS

1. Child Tax Credit
- Up to $2,000 per qualifying child under 17
- $1,700 may be refundable (Additional Child Tax Credit)
- Phases out at higher income levels

2. Child and Dependent Care Credit
- 20-35% of qualifying expenses
- Maximum expenses: $3,000 for one dependent, $6,000 for two or more
- Dependent must be under 13 or disabled

3. American Opportunity Tax Credit
- Up to $2,500 per eligible student
- For first four years of higher education
- 40% is refundable (up to $1,000)

4. Lifetime Learning Credit
- Up to $2,000 per tax return
- 20% of first $10,000 in qualified expenses
- No limit on number of years
- Not refundable

5. Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC)
- Refundable credit for low to moderate income workers
- Amount depends on income and number of children
- Maximum credit varies by filing status and dependents

6. Retirement Savings Contributions Credit (Saver's Credit)
- 10%, 20%, or 50% of retirement contributions
- Up to $1,000 credit ($2,000 if married filing jointly)
- Subject to income limits

BUSINESS DEDUCTIONS

1. Home Office Deduction
- Simplified method: $5 per square foot, up to 300 sq ft
- Actual expense method: percentage of home expenses

2. Business Vehicle Expenses
- Standard mileage rate for 2024: 67 cents per mile
- Actual expense method: depreciation, gas, repairs, insurance

3. Business Meals and Entertainment
- Generally 50% deductible
- 100% deductible for certain employee meals

4. Professional Development
- Training, conferences, professional memberships
- Books, subscriptions, continuing education
