IRS Publication 17 - Your Federal Income Tax (For Individuals)

CHAPTER 1 - FILING INFORMATION

Who Must File
You must file a federal income tax return if your income is above certain amounts. These amounts depend on your filing status, age, and the type of income you have.

Filing Status
Your filing status determines your filing requirements, standard deduction, and tax liability. There are five filing statuses:
1. Single
2. Married Filing Jointly
3. Married Filing Separately
4. Head of Household
5. Qualifying Widow(er) with Dependent Child

Standard Deduction for 2024
- Single: $14,600
- Married Filing Jointly: $29,200
- Married Filing Separately: $14,600
- Head of Household: $21,900
- Qualifying Widow(er): $29,200

Additional Standard Deduction
If you are 65 or older or blind, you may be entitled to an additional standard deduction.

CHAPTER 12 - OTHER INCOME

Canceled Debt
If a debt you owe is canceled or forgiven, other than as a gift or bequest, you generally must include the canceled amount in your income.

Gambling Winnings
You must include your gambling winnings in your income. If you receive a Form W-2G, the payer has already reported your winnings to the IRS.

CHAPTER 21 - ITEMIZED DEDUCTIONS

Medical and Dental Expenses
You can deduct medical and dental expenses that exceed 7.5% of your adjusted gross income.

State and Local Taxes
You can deduct state and local income taxes, sales taxes, and property taxes, subject to a $10,000 limit ($5,000 if married filing separately).

Charitable Contributions
You can deduct charitable contributions to qualified organizations, subject to certain limits based on your adjusted gross income.

CHAPTER 24 - TAX CREDITS

Child Tax Credit
For 2024, the Child Tax Credit is up to $2,000 per qualifying child under age 17. The credit begins to phase out for higher income taxpayers.

Earned Income Tax Credit
The Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC) is a refundable credit for working people with low to moderate income.

American Opportunity Tax Credit
The American Opportunity Tax Credit is available for qualified education expenses for the first four years of higher education.
